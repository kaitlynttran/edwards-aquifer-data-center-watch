Organizations

Other groups doing related work on data centers, water, and the Edwards Aquifer, plus the agencies that regulate them.
Greater Edwards Aquifer Alliance (GEAA)
Nonprofit coalition of 51 member organizations advocating for protection of the Edwards and Trinity aquifers across a 21-county service area. Published "Data Centers in Texas: A Review and Call for Innovation and Regulation," April 2026.
aquiferalliance.org
Edwards Aquifer Authority (EAA)
Regulatory authority managing groundwater withdrawal permits for the San Antonio segment of the Edwards Aquifer.
edwardsaquifer.org
Texas Commission on Environmental Quality (TCEQ)
State agency administering the Edwards Aquifer Protection Program and water quality regulation statewide.
tceq.texas.gov
Texas Water Development Board (TWDB)
State water planning agency; source for groundwater levels, aquifer data, and the State Water Plan.
twdb.texas.gov
Public Utility Commission of Texas (PUC)
Now surveying data centers and crypto-mining facilities statewide on water use, cooling type, and power source.
puc.texas.gov
UT Austin COMPASS
"Water Use Requirements for Data Centers in Texas," a white paper on evolving water demand from data center infrastructure.
beg.utexas.edu
Preliminary screening, published June 2026
