Kaitlyn Tran is an independent student researcher specializing in urban hydrogeology. Her work focuses on developing GIS mapping to predict groundwater contamination patterns over the Edwards Aquifer, and her research has informed policymakers working to improve groundwater regulations and sustainable land use in Central Texas. This year, she will present her work at eight conferences, including international audiences in Japan (JpGU-AGU) and Switzerland (Eurokarst). She looks forward to joining AGU's Voices for Science cohort to advocate for science-protective legislation in Washington, D.C., and to continuing her studies in karst hydrogeology to help address the groundwater crisis in Texas.
